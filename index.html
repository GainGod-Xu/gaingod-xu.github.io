Academic Profile

Hao grew up in Dongying city and finished primary school, middle school, and high school in his hometown. In 2011, he moved to Beijing to pursue his undergraduate studies. After four years at China Agricultural University (CAU), he received B. S. in Applied Chemistry and B. E. in Finance.

In 2015, he decided to pursue a master's degree in Computational Chemistry, then joined Southern Methodist University (SMU). During two years of research training and coursework in Computational Chemistry, he realized his interest in data science and programming. He started with open courses in the fields, then enrolled Online Master of Science in Computer Science (OMSCS) at Georgia Institute of Technology (GT). Now, he is wrapping up his OMSCS and continuing to finish his Ph. D. in Computational Chemistry at Boston College.

Ph. D. in Computational Chemistry

Thesis in Progress: Computational Investigation of Ligand effects and Reaction Mechanism on Amination and Hydroxylation Reaction.

The main focus is to compute and examine the reaction pathways, to visualize the interaction between catalysts and substrates and to design effective catalysts with regards to the regioselectivity, chemoselectivity and enantioselectivity, and to design the effective catalyst for amination and hydroxylation reactions with Ab initio calculations. In addition, the energy profile, radical translocation and charger transfer along reaction pathway were investigated to have a deep understanding of elemental radical reactions. The root-mean-square deviation (RMSD) and geometric operations of reaction intermediate and transition state were utilized for the categorization of transition state and energy decomposition along with machine learning algorithm.
