About Me

Dr. Hao Xu is a dedicated researcher specializing in AI-driven drug discovery and computational chemistry. Currently, he is a Research Fellow at Brigham and Women's Hospital and Harvard Medical School, focusing on developing AI-powered models for cardiovascular disease treatment.

Dr. Xu holds a PhD in Chemistry from Boston College, where he conducted research on organic synthesis and quantum chemistry. He also completed a Master's degree in Computational Chemistry at Southern Methodist University and a Master's degree in Computer Science at the Georgia Institute of Technology. His academic journey began with a Bachelor of Science in Chemistry and Finance at China Agricultural University.

Throughout his career, Dr. Xu has applied machine learning, graph neural networks, and other AI techniques to tackle complex chemical challenges, aiming to bridge the gap between computational methodologies and clinical applications.
